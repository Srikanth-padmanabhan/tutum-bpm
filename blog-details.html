Beat the Heat & the Bite: London’s Summer Pest-Control Guide

  • 1 July 2025

Summer in London and the Southeast brings warm weather and a surge in pest activity. Fleas, ants, wasps, and flies breed rapidly, while rodents move indoors searching for food. For commercial properties, prevention is critical.

Rodents: Rats and mice are the most common summer invaders, damaging property and spreading disease. Even a single sighting can cause serious compliance and reputational issues.

Wasps: Colonies grow rapidly in summer, becoming aggressive by late season. Early nest removal is essential to avoid stings and disruption.

Birds: Pigeons & Gulls

Pigeons and gulls roost on rooftops and balconies, damaging buildings and posing health risks. Gulls can also become aggressive during nesting season.

Bedbugs

Bedbugs spread quickly during peak travel months. Early detection prevents costly closures and treatments.

Flies & Fruit Flies

Flies breed rapidly in waste areas and contaminate food surfaces, becoming a major hygiene risk in summer.

Your Summer Pest-Proof Plan

Seal entry points, manage waste, maintain hygiene, and schedule regular inspections. Prevention stops small issues becoming big problems.
